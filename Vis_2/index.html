An overview of Apple's and Samsung's global performance

Who is dominating the global market?

Apple Fact

Apple's iOS operating system, which is exclusive to its iPhone devices, is known for its seamless integration with other Apple products and services, such as the Mac, iPad, Apple Watch, and iCloud. Which limits its appeal to richer nations.

Samsung Fact

Samsung has a significant market share and a wide range of smartphone models catering to various consumer preferences and price points, from premium flagship devices to mid-range and budget options.

So who is generating more revenue?

Apple's Soaring Growth:

Apple's revenue has shown remarkable growth over the years, particularly from 2004 onward, with a staggering 81.40% increase in 2010 and a subsequent 67.59% increase in 2011.

Samsung's Fluctuations:

Samsung's revenue, while generally increasing, has been more volatile. The company experienced both positive and negative revenue changes, notably a 25.80% increase in 2012 and a -10.67% decrease in 2019.

Market domination is not everything

Apple

  • Apple's market capitalization also exhibits fluctuations over the years, but with a general upward trend.
  • The market cap was highest in 2022 at $2,774 billion, and it was lowest in 2001 at $5.16 billion.
  • Apple had a remarkable growth year in 2004, with a 230.6% increase in market cap, largely due to the release of the iPod and the introduction of iTunes.
  • In 2008, Apple experienced a significant decrease of 56.33% in market cap, likely due to the global financial crisis.
  • In recent years, Apple's market cap has been consistently increasing, with a 34.25% increase in 2023.

Samsung

  • The market capitalization of Samsung has seen fluctuations over the years, with both increases and decreases.
  • The market cap was highest in 2020 at $500.83 billion, and it was lowest in 2011 at $130.66 billion.
  • The company experienced significant market cap growth in 2009, with a 95.41% increase, which is the highest annual growth in the dataset.
  • The company experienced its biggest drop in market cap in 2008, with a decrease of 41.33%.
  • In recent years, Samsung's market cap has been recovering, with a 9.89% increase in 2023.
